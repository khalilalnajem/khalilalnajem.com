Who I Am

I'm Khalil, a Kuwaiti student at the University of Glasgow, finishing a degree in Business & Management while building companies and thinking seriously about how knowledge gets made and taught. I started at Colorado State University, transferred to Glasgow, and rebuilt how I work along the way.

Along the way, I've been an aviation content creator, a teenage brand builder, a Mindvalley student, including a personal branding quest with Beto Perez (Zumba founder, Babson alumnus). Every phase taught me something I couldn't have learned in a classroom.

My work sits at the intersection of entrepreneurship, AI, and education. I'm not interested in the surface layer of business: the pitch decks, the hustle rhetoric, the tidy frameworks taught without context. I'm interested in what actually drives decisions under uncertainty, and how we might teach that more honestly.

I've been building since I was sixteen: freelance design, iOS apps, an AI education platform for the Gulf, a national competition award for an LLM-powered product. My dissertation asks whether large language models can genuinely support the way founders think, not automate it, but augment it. 64 journal articles reviewed through PRISMA methodology.

Teaching has always pulled at me. As a child, I gathered my siblings to play school. When my father asked what gift I wanted, I chose a whiteboard. At university, friends come to me because they know I'll help them think, not just give answers. I don't see teaching as separate from entrepreneurship. For me, it's the highest form of it: you take what you know and make it usable for someone else.

After Glasgow, I'm pursuing a Masters in Entrepreneurship, then a PhD. My long-term goal is to become a professor who bridges entrepreneurship research, creative action, and education. I want to bring rigorous, research-grounded entrepreneurship education back to Kuwait, in Arabic, embedded in Gulf context, and free from the mythology that surrounds the field.
